Dealing with Procrastination.



Newton's 1st Law
I've noticed that the 1st law of motion is easily applicable to productivity.
For those who don't know it:

"An object at rest will continue to stay at rest and an object travelling at a constant velocity will continue at that constant velocity, until an external force acts upon it."

This means that once you are moving, you'll continue moving without needing any force applied.
So in terms of handling procrastination, the main thing we need to do is to get started.
A big part of productivity is hacking our brains to take off. The method I use to implement this is called the "Two-minute rule" by David Allen.
Remove as much friction that stops you from starting. Place cues that make it easier to complete your task.


Flow State
The flow state is the deepest focus achievable. It's the state where hours can fly past unnoticed.
When we are fully relaxed, we can't enter the flow state. It has to be a challenging task. We have to remove distractions (internally and externally).
Set cues that trigger your flow state. Maybe a song that you only listen to while doing your deep work (use instrumental music).
We don't notice when we enter the flow state.
