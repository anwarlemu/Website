How I Missed A $20,000 Opportunity.



I strongly believe that the best way to become financially free is to gain multiple sources of income. One of my streams is in the music industry as a "Ghost Producer". I produce instrumentals that singers and video makers use (without my name being included in the song details). I've been doing this for quite some time now, (since 2015) but didn't start making high returns until 2020.


What Happened

I usually sell my instrumentals in terms of percentage. I collect 10% of whatever profit you make for the first month of your release.

A few months ago (around November), I sold an instrumental to a Japanese artist called Eve. I made the sale and minded my own business.

On January 7th, one of my favourite Anime, The Promised Neverland, released the second season of their series. While watching episode 2 yesterday, (January 15th) I recognised the instrumental of the ending soundtrack but couldn't pinpoint where I heard it from. I then decided to do some further inspection on the song. After a few hours of meta tracking, I discovered that the song was done by a Japanese singer called Myuk. I was about to leave the website when something caught my eye. The person in charge of the songwriting and production unit was called Eve. I spent the next two hours looking for the name of the instrumental producer. The instrumental for the song was made by a Ghost Producer.

The song has made a total of around $200,000 (as of January 16th) since the time of release. That would mean that I would collect a 10% gain, making it a $20,000 gain for me. Not really.

In August, I decided the change my method of selling from percentage-based to a fixed price. Meaning I only made $30 (which I sold it for).

Nevertheless, I really enjoy listening to what my little song grew up to become.
