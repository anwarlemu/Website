THE OTHER SIDE OF PRODUCTIVITY


I’m amazed by how many people think that seeking productivity boosts will instantly improve your life. We’re obsessed with it because so many of us directly associate it with our own sense of self-worth.

Unfortunately, “productivity” isn't an ultimate road to success and happiness. We’re already unbelievably more productive than all of our ancestors. Sending or receiving messages takes seconds now, not days or weeks or months like it used to.

I believe this society-wide drive to be more productive comes from a place of dissatisfaction, because you wouldn’t care about it if you already feel fully satisfied with the quality of efficiency of what you do in life.

If you never explore why you’re seeking more productivity or efficiency or hustle or whatever it is you're aiming for, then it will never be enough. The answer isn’t because you "just need to pass the next hurdle", because there will always be more hurdles.

You need to find why you seek productivity. Adding that sense of purpose to your work would multiply its value (for both you and your clients).
