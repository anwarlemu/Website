Lessons from Japan

I had the privelege of learning about Japan and it's culture for the last few months. I got a pretty good idea of what living among Japanese was really like.
I learned so much and I am very happy to be able to share a few lessons with you

Go the Extra Mile

In Japan, when you ask for directions to a place, people would usually accompany you to the place to make sure you don't get lost. They would fully pause what they are doing just to help other people.
Help people and show respect to others.



Don't Worry About What You Can't Control

The commonly used Japanese phrase "Sho ga nai." It basically means, don't worry about what what isn't in your control.

Many people (including me at times) worry too much about situations out of our control and it ends up just giving us even more stress. We shouldn't worry ourselves on these types of things.
Forget about those useless problems and enjoy the moment. Be present. Never miss what is happening now.
