Living More with Less.



Minimalism is living with more by choosing to have less of what doesn't matter.


Common Myths About Minimalism

1. Minimalism means getting rid of everything.
Minimalism doesn't mean you need to throw everything out and sleep in an empty room everyday. It is decluttering the things that we don't need (things that don't bring value into our lives).

In my case, I got rid of things that I didn't need such as my bed, excess clothes and many other things that didn't need to have. Even though, I wear the same clothes everyday, I still have a colourful bullet journal and over 30 different coloured highlighters.

To live a minimalist life, you need to get rid of the items that you don't need (including that shirt you never wear).

You can declutter using the "30-30 rule":

"If you haven't used something in the last thirty days, and you don't need it for the next thirty days, declutter it (if it isn't an emergency or medical item)."


2. Minimalists have to have monotone things.

No. I have a notebook full of colours and I highlight all my notes for school. You can have colour in your life if that's what brings value.


Why Minimalism
- When you don't have worthless things to worry about, you have extra mental clarity and can focus more on more meaningful things.
- It takes more energy to maintain more than to enjoy less.
- Being present. When you live with things that give value, there's more time to live now.
